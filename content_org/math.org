 During the study of linear algebra, several asistant materials are
 referenced:
1. The opencourse 18.06 from [[http://www-math.mit.edu/~gs/][Gilbert Strang]]. From Strang's home, many
   valuable materials are available.
2. The textbook /Introduction to Linear Algebra/ by Gilbert Strang.
3. The textbook /Linear Algebra Done Right/ by [[http://www.axler.net/][Sheldon Axler]]. You can
   get an abridged version of /Linear Algebra Done Right/ from Axler's
   homepage.
4. The textbook /Matrix Analysis and Applied Linear Algebra/ by [[http://meyer.math.ncsu.edu/][Carl D. Meyer]].

By following Professor Strang, I got an overview of the four spaces of
one matrix. Actually, I learnt the 18.06 from the MIT OCW more than
one time. After each time, my understanding of linear algebra was
deepened.
